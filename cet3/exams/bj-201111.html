2011年11月北京地区成人本科学士学位英语统一考试真题

Part I Reading Comprehension
Passage 1
The reflective towers of New York City, which is on the Atlantic migrating(迁徙的) route, can be deadly for birds. "We live in an age of glass," said Ms. Laurel, an architect. (76) "It can be a perfect mirror in certain lights, and the larger the glass, the more dangerous it is." About 90,000 birds are killed by flying into buildings in the city each year. Often, they strike the lower levels of glass towers after searching for food in nearby parks. Such crashes are the second-leading cause of death for migrating birds, after habitat(栖息地) loss, with an estimated number of death ranging up to a billion a year.
位于大西洋迁徙路线上的纽约市，其反光的高楼大厦对鸟类来说是致命的。“我们生活在一个玻璃时代，”建筑师劳雷尔女士说。“在某些光线条件下，玻璃可以成为完美的镜子，而且玻璃面积越大，危险性就越高。”每年大约有9万只鸟因撞上城市的建筑物而死亡。它们通常在附近公园觅食后，会撞击到玻璃塔楼的低层。这类撞击事件是候鸟死亡的第二大原因，仅次于栖息地丧失，估计每年有多达10亿只鸟因此丧生。
(77) As glass office and apartment towers have increased in the last decade, so, too, have calls to make them less deadly to birds. San Francisco adopted bird-safety standards for new buildings in July. The United States Green Building Council, a nonprofit industry group that encourages the creation of environmentally conscious buildings, will introduce a bird-safety credit this fall as part of its environmental certification process.
在过去十年中，随着玻璃办公楼和公寓楼的增多，要求降低它们对鸟类致命性的呼声也日益高涨。今年7月，旧金山采纳了针对新建建筑的鸟类安全标准。美国绿色建筑委员会，一个鼓励创建环保建筑的非营利行业组织，将于今年秋季在其环境认证过程中引入鸟类安全加分项。
There are no easy fixes, however. A few researchers are exploring glass designs that use ultraviolet(紫外线的) signals, but they are still in their infancy. Covers, dot patterns, shades and nets are the main options available.
然而，没有简单的解决办法。少数研究人员正在探索使用紫外线信号的玻璃设计，但这些技术仍处于起步阶段。目前可用的主要方案包括覆盖物、点状图案、遮阳板和网罩。
Often, only one section of a building needs to be changed. "You don't necessarily have to treat every window," Ms. Laurel said. "It would be too expensive to do the whole building." The Jacob K. Javits Convention Center, which has been undergoing alterations, is the most recent building to voluntarily correct the problem of bird crashes. The architects used less reflective glass and dot patterns.
通常，只需要改变建筑物的某一部分。“你不必处理每一扇窗户，”劳雷尔女士说。“整栋楼都这样做的话成本太高了。”近期，正在进行改建的雅各布·K·贾维茨会议中心是自愿纠正鸟类撞击问题的最新建筑。建筑师们使用了反射性较低的玻璃并加入了点状图案。
